Pre approval vs pre qualification

Both help you plan your budget, but only pre approval gives you verified buying power. Knowing the difference can save time and make your offer stand out.

Pre qualification: an estimate

Pre qualification is a quick overview of what you might afford. It uses self reported income and debt and does not verify documents. It’s helpful early when you’re exploring options.

Pre approval: verified confidence

A pre approval means a lender has reviewed your credit, income, and assets. You’ll get a letter confirming the loan amount and terms you qualify for. Sellers and agents treat this as proof you’re ready to buy.

Why it matters

In competitive markets, pre approval can be the difference between your offer being accepted or skipped. It tells sellers your financing is real, not just theoretical.
