How to price your home

The best price is a range supported by recent sales, current inventory, and condition. The goal is strong traffic and serious offers in a reasonable time for your area.

Start with the right comps

Look at similar homes that closed recently. Focus on size, bed and bath count, lot type, age, and neighborhood features.

Adjust for differences

Premium lots, new roofs, updated kitchens, and pools can influence buyer demand. Dated systems or deferred maintenance can push value down.

Match the market tempo

Watch median days on market and list to sale ratios. If inventory is tight, pricing near top of range can still work. With more supply, aim mid range to draw more showings.
