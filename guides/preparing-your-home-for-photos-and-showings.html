Preparing your home for photos and showings

Buyers shop with their eyes first. A light, tidy home with clear rooms brings more traffic and stronger offers. This plan keeps it simple.

One week before photos

  • Declutter countertops, closets, and garage to show space.
  • Touch up paint and repair obvious wear.
  • Service lawn, trim bushes, and add fresh mulch for curb appeal.

The day of photos

  • Open blinds, turn on all lights, and hide cords and trash bins.
  • Remove car from driveway and tidy porch and entry.
  • Set table or add a simple vase for warmth.

For every showing

  • Air out rooms, set a comfortable temperature, and secure pets.
  • Leave a brief features sheet with recent upgrades and utility info.
  • Exit five minutes early so buyers feel relaxed to explore.
