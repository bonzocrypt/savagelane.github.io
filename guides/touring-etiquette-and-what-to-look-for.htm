Touring etiquette and what to look for

Touring is quick. Keep it respectful, focused, and consistent so you do not miss red flags or rare wins.

Etiquette basics

  • Arrive on time and follow showing instructions.
  • Limit photos and commentary if the seller is present.
  • Leave the home as you found it and lock up as instructed.

Ten minute check

  • Roof age, HVAC age, electrical panel, and visible plumbing.
  • Windows, water pressure, and signs of past leaks.
  • Street noise, yard grade, and HOA or parking realities.

Keep a consistent scorecard

  1. Use the same checklist for every home you tour.
  2. Note deal breakers and top three pros on the spot.
  3. Compare later with clear heads and real numbers.
