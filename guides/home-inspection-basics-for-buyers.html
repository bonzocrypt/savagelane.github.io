Home inspection basics for buyers

The goal is a safe and sound home at a fair price. Use this to focus on the big items and decide when a repair, credit, or walk away makes sense.

What inspectors check

  • Roof, structure, foundation, and drainage.
  • HVAC, electrical, plumbing, and major appliances.
  • Safety items like GFCI, railings, and smoke detectors.

How to read the report

  • Separate safety and system issues from cosmetic notes.
  • Ask for estimates before requesting large credits.
  • Confirm any issues that may affect insurance or loan approval.

Next steps

  1. Get quotes for serious items and decide on repair or credit.
  2. Use contract timelines and keep scope clear in writing.
  3. Balance fixes with market reality if the home is a rare fit.
