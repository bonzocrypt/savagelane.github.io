Occupancy and rent back options

Need time after closing to move or finish your next home. A short rent back can work well when you set clear terms and protect both sides.

Common options

  • Post-closing occupancy You stay in the home for a set period after closing and pay a daily rate.
  • Leaseback A short written lease that defines rent, deposit, utilities, and maintenance.
  • Holdover buffer A limited grace period with a higher per diem to discourage delays.

What to set in writing

  • Exact dates and move-out time, daily rate, and deposit amount.
  • Responsibility for utilities, lawn, pool, and minor repairs.
  • Insurance requirements and buyer access for appraisals or work.

Risk checks

  1. Confirm buyer’s loan allows post-closing occupancy timeline.
  2. Verify insurance coverage for both parties during the period.
  3. Photograph condition at key handoffs and attach to the agreement.
